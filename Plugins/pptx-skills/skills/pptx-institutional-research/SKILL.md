# PPTX Institutional Research

Read `pptx-institutional-deck` first. Then read the matching orientation skill. This skill adds research-content conventions only; it does not introduce any alternate visual system.

## When To Use

Use this skill for:

- equity research decks
- IC memos
- diligence summaries
- market-update decks
- comps and valuation presentations
- management or board materials that need an institutional research tone

## Research Content Rules

- Titles should state the conclusion, not just the topic.
- Every quantitative slide needs `Source:` and an as-of date when timing matters.
- Units and currencies must be explicit.
- Avoid unsourced superlatives and invented ratings, estimates, or disclosures.
- Keep commentary analytical and compact.

## Recommended Deck Structure

1. Cover
2. Disclaimer when required
3. Executive summary
4. Investment thesis
5. Company or asset overview
6. Market / industry
7. Financial performance
8. Peers / comps
9. Valuation
10. Risks
11. Catalysts / timeline
12. Summary / recommendation
13. Appendix

Adapt the order to the assignment, but keep the tone analytical and restrained.

## Visual Routing For Research Slides

Use the specialist skill that matches the analytical need:

- ranked sector performance or peer ranking: `pptx-chart-column`
- long-label exposure or positioning comparison: `pptx-chart-bar`
- segmented contribution or mix bridge: `pptx-chart-stacked-column`
- time-series factor, spread, or price history: `pptx-chart-line`
- trend plus scale emphasis: `pptx-chart-area`
- simple mix or share: `pptx-chart-pie` or `pptx-chart-donut`
- factor or valuation relationship: `pptx-chart-scatter`
- exposure matrix, summary table, comps, or return table: `pptx-visual-table`
- narrative value build from AI / ML or strategic initiatives: `pptx-visual-growth-story`

## Research-Specific Table And Valuation Rules

- Keep comps and valuation tables readable before making them dense.
- Show methodology labels explicitly: `DCF`, `Comps`, `SOTP`, or scenario names.
- Separate `Source:` from `Note:` when both are needed.
- Use muted emphasis for downside and upside markers; keep them sparse.
- Accept higher information density only when hierarchy remains clear.

## Risks, Catalysts, And Disclosure

- Risks should be specific and measurable where possible.
- Catalysts should be tied to dates, reporting events, launches, approvals, or financing steps.
- If disclosure language is required, place it on a dedicated disclaimer slide.
- Keep footnotes tight but readable.

## QA Checklist

Before delivery, confirm:

- thesis-led titles are used on the main analytical slides
- all quantitative claims are sourced
- valuation slides show methodology and timing
- risks and catalysts are concrete
- required disclaimers are included
- the deck still fully complies with `pptx-institutional-deck`
